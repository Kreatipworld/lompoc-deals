# The Placement Methodology — A Guide for Marketing Partners

*How Lompoc Locals is built to sell, and how you sell it.*

**Lompoc Locals — the #1 local hub for Lompoc, CA.** Connecting locals, businesses, and visitors across Lompoc + Vandenberg (ZIPs 93436/37/38).

This document is for marketing vendors, agencies, and resellers who want to sell or promote Lompoc Locals partnerships. It explains what we actually sell, why it converts in a small town, and how to package and pitch it.

---

## The core idea: we are a placement seller

Most "directory" products sell a *listing* — a page that sits there. We don't. **The hub is made of spaces.** Every high-traffic surface on the site — the homepage, category pages, the map, search results, the bottom of every profile, blog, event, and garage-sale page — contains defined placements where a member's brand can appear.

A membership doesn't buy a page. **A membership fills those spaces with the member's presence.** That's the whole model in one line:

> **We sell attention; partners fill it.**

And the mechanics are generous by design: **one membership lights up the whole site.** A business doesn't buy the homepage marquee, then the map marker, then the search row, then the featured-deals strip separately. They step up one tier and *every* placement turns on at once. That makes the offer easy to explain and easy to close — the value is visible everywhere, immediately.

## Why this converts in a small town

Lompoc is a real place where business owners know each other. That's exactly why the model works. Three forces do the selling:

**1. Trust engine — the badge.** The Official Partner badge is a visible mark of credibility on a hub locals already use to decide where to go. In a town this size, being the vetted, badged name in your category is social proof that compounds.

**2. Growth engine — FOMO from contrast.** Walk any category page and you see the difference immediately: badged Partners sit up top, branded and prominent; un-badged businesses sit below, plain. That contrast is the pitch. A business owner scrolling their own category and seeing a competitor's gold-star marker on the map understands the stakes without a slide deck.

**3. Scarcity product — Category Exclusive.** One per category. It's not a marketing claim; it's **database-enforced** — the slot physically cannot be oversold. When it's gone, it's gone, and no competitor can buy past it. Scarcity you can't fake is the easiest premium tier there is to sell.

Trust brings them in, FOMO moves them up the ladder, scarcity closes the top of the ladder. You're not convincing people the town is worth reaching — they already know that. You're showing them the seat is filling.

## How to pitch and package it

**Lead with the contrast, not the feature list.** Open on the prospect's own category page. Show them who's badged and who isn't. Let the map do the talking. The product demonstrates itself.

**Sell the ladder, not a single price.** The move is almost always *up one rung*:
- A cold prospect → **claim Free** (get them on the map; now they have something to lose).
- A Free business running any promotion → **Growth** (deals + digest + analytics pay for themselves).
- A Growth business that wants to be *the* name → **Plus / Official Partner** (badge, first place, site-wide reach — one step, whole site lights up).
- A Plus Partner worried about a rival → **Category Exclusive** (lock the category; no one can follow).

**Package suggestions vendors can resell:**
- *Own-Your-Category* — Category Exclusive on annual ($2,490/yr) as a done-for-you positioning play for a category leader.
- *Get-Found Starter* — Growth + the done-for-you curated profile upgrade path, aimed at businesses with weak online presence.
- *Launch-Day* — tie a Plus partnership to the events hub and Vandenberg rocket-launch traffic for visibility-hungry brands.

**Objection handling is built in.** "Is it worth it?" → point at the badged Partners already there. "Can I wait?" → Category Exclusive is one-per-category and filling. "How much work is it for me?" → the curated profile is done-for-you; they approve, we build.

## What makes the inventory defensible

For a reseller, the important question is whether this is a durable thing to sell. It is, for four reasons:

1. **Enforced scarcity.** Category Exclusive is one per category, enforced in the database — it literally cannot be oversold. That's a premium SKU competitors can't undercut because there's no second seat to sell.
2. **Curated quality.** Partner profiles are done-for-you and curated — real photos, verified details. The hub looks credible, so the badge means something. A junk directory can't replicate trust.
3. **The hub position.** Lompoc Locals is the local-only hub for one town — the directory, the interactive map, the events calendar (including Vandenberg rocket launches), curated content, and the weekly digest in one place. That local word-of-mouth position is hard to clone and easy to sell against generic national platforms.
4. **Finite inventory.** Ten categories, one Exclusive seat each. The supply is fixed and visible. Fixed supply plus visible demand is the cleanest possible sales story.

Nine founding Official Partners are already live. Every open category is a seat someone in that line of work will eventually take — the vendor's job is simply to make sure the prospect understands that before their competitor does.
