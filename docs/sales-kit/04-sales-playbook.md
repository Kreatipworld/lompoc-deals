# Lompoc Locals — The Sales Playbook

*The definitive guide to winning members. Door-to-door, phone/DM, and online.*

---

## What we are (say it this way, every time)

**Lompoc Locals is the #1 local hub for Lompoc — connecting locals, businesses, and visitors.** Lompoc and Vandenberg only. Nothing else.

We're not "another website" you have to build and babysit. We're the place people already come to find what's happening in town — the directory, the map, the events, the deals — and we save you a curated spot in it.

### The one thing to internalize
We sell **attention**, not software. The hub has prime spaces — the top of a category, a pin on the map, a featured coupon, a badge that says *Official Partner*. A member fills those spaces. That's the whole product.

Two forces do the selling for us:
- **Trust** comes from the **Official Partner badge**. It's the "this business is legit and part of the community" signal.
- **New members** come from **FOMO** — the visible contrast between the badged, featured Partners at the top and everyone else sitting plain and unclaimed below them.

Our job isn't to convince. It's to *show* — and let the contrast do the talking.

### The voice — non-negotiable
Neighborly. Warm. Word-of-mouth. **Sell without the urge.** We're a neighbor telling another neighbor about something good, not a rep working a quota. If you ever feel pushy, slow down. The demo persuades; you don't have to.

Bilingual — run the whole thing in English or Spanish, whatever puts the owner at ease.

This is the move that wins. Owners are busy and skeptical of "one more thing to manage." So we remove the effort entirely.

**We build their page for them before we even ask for money:**

So the close isn't "here's what we could build." It's **"here's your page — it's already live and it already looks great. Want to keep it?"** You pull it up on your phone. That's the moment.

# THE LIVE DEMO FLOW

This is the heart of the sale. Do it in this exact order. Every step raises the temperature.

**1. Their profile first.**
Open straight to *their* already-built page. "This is you — it's live right now." Let them see real photos of their own business, their logo, the write-up. This disarms the "another website / more work" reflex instantly, because the work is already done.

**2. The badge.**
Scroll to a page with the **Official Partner** badge. "See this? That's the trust mark. When a local sees it, they know you're the real, verified, part-of-the-community business — not a random listing." Point at it. Let it register.

**3. The map marker.**
Pull up the interactive map. "Here's the whole town. Every pin is a business people can find. Here's where you sit." Make it tangible — they're *on the map* of their own town.

**4. The category slide.**
Now the FOMO. Open their category. Scroll it slowly. "Here's [category] in Lompoc. The Partner up top gets the eyes. Everyone under them blends together." Let them watch themselves sit in the crowd — then say: "I'd rather put you up here." Slide back to the top.

**5. Featured deals.**
Finish on the deals/coupon view. "And this is where your coupon lives — the thing that actually walks someone through your door." Show a live featured coupon. End on the picture of a customer arriving.

**The close is baked into the flow:** by step 5 they've seen their own beautiful page, understood the badge, found themselves on the map, felt the contrast in their category, and pictured a customer. You barely have to ask.

# OBJECTION HANDLING

Warm and honest, always. Never argue — agree, then reframe.

### "I'm too busy."
> "Totally — that's exactly why I built the page *for* you first. There's genuinely nothing for you to do. No forms, no uploads, no login to babysit. I did all of it. You literally just tell me to keep it live. Being busy is the reason to say yes, not no."

### "I already have Facebook / a website."
> "Good — keep both. This isn't instead of those. Facebook is where your *followers* are; Lompoc Locals is where people who *don't know you yet* are looking — the map, the directory, the events, folks new to town and coming for the launches. Different audience. And honestly, your page here is already built and looks great — want to see how it sits next to it?"

### "It's too expensive."
> "Fair — let's find the right fit. Growth is $39.99 and gets you a proper page and a coupon. The Official Partner badge is $99.99 — that's the trust mark and the top spot. Think about one customer the coupon brings in; that usually covers it. Want to start at the level that feels comfortable and move up when you see it working?"

### "Does it actually work?"
> "Honest answer: here's what it is and isn't. We're small on email still, so I won't promise you inbox blasts. What you're getting is your spot in the place people already browse — 476 businesses, the map, 96 events including the rocket launches. Nine businesses already went Partner — One Plant, Vargas, In&Out Tires — because being the one people find first in your category is worth it. It works the way a good corner storefront works: visibility where people are already looking."

### "Let me think about it."
> "Of course — no pressure at all, that's not how we do things. The page stays live either way, so you can sit with it. The only thing I'd flag: the top spot in your category is a *one-per-category* thing. If a competitor locks it in, it's gone. Want me to hold your category spot for a few days while you think, so the decision's still yours to make?"

# BEACHHEAD FIRST

Don't sell in random order. Start where the demo lands hardest and the FOMO compounds.

**Target first:**
- **Warm** businesses — owners who already like the idea of community, or came from a referral.
- **Well-photographed** businesses — the ones whose page will look *stunning* the moment you pull it up. The photo is the pitch.
- **Competitive categories** — where "the only one at the top" actually means something, and where one signed Partner makes every competitor next door feel the pressure.

Land those first. Each beautiful, badged Partner you sign becomes proof for the next pitch — and the visible contrast that makes the next owner in that category want in. Build the top of the categories, and the middle sells itself.
