# Lompoc Locals — Brand Guidelines

*Practical rules for keeping the brand consistent, warm, and unmistakably local.*

---

## The logo & the mark

Our identity is anchored by the **"L" mark**: a white **L** paired with a **gold sun** and a **green leaf** — a small symbol of a sunny, growing, close-knit town.

**Asset files** (live in `/public/brand/`):

| File | Use it for |
|---|---|
| `lompoc-locals-logo.svg` | Primary full logo — headers, hero, letterhead, decks |
| `lompoc-locals-mark.svg` | The standalone mark on light backgrounds |
| `lompoc-locals-mark-white.svg` | The standalone mark on dark or photographic backgrounds |

**Do**
- Give the logo clear space on all sides — at least the height of the sun element.
- Use the SVGs so the mark stays crisp at any size.
- Use the white mark over photos, purple fields, or dark UI.

**Don't**
- Recolor, stretch, rotate, or add effects (shadows, gradients, outlines).
- Place the light mark on a busy or low-contrast background.
- Rebuild the mark — always use the supplied files.

## Color palette

Three brand colors carry the identity. Purple leads; green and gold support and accent.

| Color | Hex | Role |
|---|---|---|
| **Brand Purple** | `#650C75` | Primary. Headers, key UI, the "L" stem, brand fields. |
| **Brand Green** | `#0B992F` | Secondary. Growth, positive actions, the leaf, accents. |
| **Brand Gold** | `#EFC618` | Accent. Highlights, badges, the sun, small moments of warmth. |

**Usage notes**
- Lead with purple; use green and gold to support — not compete.
- Keep **status colors** (approved / pending / rejected) and **per-card category gradients** as their own system. They are functional UI, not brand color — don't force them into the palette.
- Maintain accessible contrast: purple and green work as text on white; gold is an accent, not body-text color.
- Use plenty of white/neutral space. The palette should feel bright and open, like a sunny day in town.

## Typography direction

Type should feel **clean, friendly, and highly readable** — a neighbor talking, not a corporation announcing.

- **Headings:** a warm, confident sans-serif with clear weight contrast. Approachable, never stiff.
- **Body:** a neutral, legible sans-serif that reads comfortably in both English and Spanish (mind accented characters — á, é, í, ó, ú, ñ, ¿, ¡).
- **Hierarchy:** communicate with size and weight, not decoration. Generous line spacing; comfortable measure.
- Avoid all-caps for long text, condensed display faces, and anything that reads as loud or salesy.

## Voice & tone

We are **neighborly, community-first, warm, and word-of-mouth**. We sell without the urge. Everything we publish should feel like a trusted local recommendation — an endorsement and a sense of belonging, never "buy an ad."

**Principles**
1. **Talk like a neighbor.** Plain, warm, human. Skip jargon and hype.
2. **Endorse, don't advertise.** Being featured is a mark of belonging, not a purchase.
3. **Community before commerce.** Lead with what helps locals; the business benefit follows.
4. **Bilingual and equal.** English and Spanish are first-class — not a translation afterthought.
5. **Real over hype.** Never inflate. Concrete and true beats big and vague.

**Vocabulary — say this, not that**

| Say | Not |
|---|---|
| Official Partner | advertiser / sponsor |
| Featured member | ad / placement |
| Get found by locals | buy exposure |
| Join the hub | sign up for a listing |
| Neighbors / locals | users / traffic |
| Discover | browse our inventory |

**DO / DON'T examples**

- **DO:** "Meet Casa Verde — an Official Partner locals already love."
  **DON'T:** "Casa Verde paid to advertise with us."
- **DO:** "Now featured on Lompoc Locals — find them on the map."
  **DON'T:** "Sponsored ad: click here now!"
- **DO:** "476 local businesses. One place to find them."
  **DON'T:** "The biggest, most powerful marketing platform in California."
- **DO (ES):** "Descubre lo mejor de Lompoc, en tu idioma."
  **DON'T (ES):** machine-translated copy that ignores tone or accents.

## How the brand shows up

- **Favicon / app icon:** the standalone **mark** (`lompoc-locals-mark.svg`), or `lompoc-locals-mark-white.svg` on a purple tile. Keep it simple — it must read at 16px.
- **Share / social covers:** purple field, white mark, one warm line of copy. Optional gold or green accent. Let it breathe.
- **Badges — "Official Partner" & "Featured Member":** small, tasteful marks a business can wear with pride. Use brand colors; gold reads as a seal of belonging. Never style them like a discount sticker or a hard-sell CTA.
- **Everywhere:** bilingual by default, focused on Lompoc + Vandenberg only, and always warm. If it feels like an ad, rework it until it feels like a recommendation.
