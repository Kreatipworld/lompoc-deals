# Lompoc Locals — The #1 Platform in Lompoc Plan

**Date:** 2026-07-16 · **Owner:** Andres · **Horizon:** 90 days

The goal: when anyone in Lompoc — resident, visitor, or business owner — wants to know
*what's happening locally* or wants *locals to know about them*, Lompoc Locals is the
default. Not a website they've heard of; the one they check.

The real competition is not another deals site. It's the **Lompoc Community Board
Facebook group, Nextdoor, and Google itself**. We don't out-build them; we out-local them.

---

## The three scoreboards (what "#1" means, measurably)

### 1. Search — own the Lompoc intent queries
**Target:** first organic result for "lompoc deals", "garage sales lompoc",
"things to do in lompoc", "lompoc events", "restaurants lompoc" (EN + ES).

- Foundation shipped: canonicals, hreflang, sitemap, event JSON-LD, garage-sales
  landing page, 48-post blog (see `docs/marketing/seo-playbook.md`).
- Next lever: **business profiles as the best page on the internet for each business.**
  The Beachhead-25 enrichment (photos, about, contact) makes our page richer than the
  business's own site — for several, we're their *only* real page (dead sites, FB-only).
  Google rewards that; owners claim it; backlinks follow.
- Measure: GSC positions for the 5 head terms + profile-page impressions, weekly.

### 2. Businesses — the default place to post a special
**Target:** 15 businesses live with real deals (Phase 1 goal), then every "special"
in town posts here first because it demonstrably works.

- Motion: concierge beachhead (`docs/marketing/beachhead-25.md`) — done-for-you setup,
  founding-business framing, close on the spot.
- The enriched profiles flip the pitch from "join my site" to
  **"your page already looks amazing — want the keys?"**
- Contact capture: tracker now holds emails + verified phones
  (`docs/marketing/beachhead-25-tracker.csv`); dossier holds SEO hooks + socials per
  business for spotlights and outreach.
- Measure: businesses live with an active deal; % of pitches closed; reshares per spotlight.

### 3. Locals — the audience nobody can take away
**Target:** the weekly digest is Lompoc's "what's happening this week" email.

- The email list is the moat. Facebook can throttle reach; Google can reshuffle
  rankings; the list is ours. It's also the advertiser inventory (digest ad spots are
  already priced: $49/wk, $79/mo, $99/mo).
- The FB group is a **channel, not a competitor**: post deals/events there with a link
  back, where rules allow. Drain attention, don't fight it.
- Measure: digest subscribers, open rate, weekly active visitors to /feed.

## Structural advantages to press (nobody else will bother)

1. **Spanish parity** — ~half of Lompoc is Hispanic/Latino; the ES site already exists.
   Every landing page, digest issue, and spotlight ships bilingual. No local competitor does this.
2. **Vandenberg** — launches are the highest-traffic recurring event in the county.
   Event pages + reactive social content own that search moment (kit exists:
   `marketing/social/rocket-launch-reactive-kit.md`).
3. **Events > deals for frequency** — deals bring businesses; events bring weekly
   habit. Feed both, lead marketing with events.
4. **Data quality as moat** — verified phones, real photos, current hours. Every stale
   Yelp/FB page in town makes us look better.

## Not now (deliberately)

- Paid ads (organic + concierge first; revisit at 15 live businesses).
- Expansion beyond ZIPs 93436/37/38 — Lompoc + Vandenberg only. Depth beats breadth.
- Building new product surface — distribution and density, not features, win this phase.
