# Lompoc Locals — Engagement Playbook

*How Lompoc Locals engages better: grow the local audience, and turn business owners into paying members who post deals.*

**Brand essence:** community & communication.
**Voice:** neighborly, warm, plain-spoken, proud-local, encouraging. Short sentences. Second person. No jargon, no pushy hype.
**Site:** https://www.lompoclocals.com · Business signup: `/for-businesses`

Taglines to keep handy:
- Consumer — **"All of Lompoc, in one place."**
- Community — **"Live local. Love Lompoc."**
- For businesses — **"Get found by the whole valley."**
- Shop-local — **"We're stronger when we shop local."**

---

## 1. Goals

Three concrete engagement goals. Everything in this playbook rolls up to one of them.

1. **Grow local subscribers.** Get more Lompoc and Vandenberg neighbors reading the free weekly digest and browsing deals. This is our audience and our proof to businesses.
2. **Get businesses to claim their free listing.** Every restaurant, shop, salon, auto shop, winery, and service in the valley should have a listing. Free listings build the directory and open the door to paid deals later.
3. **Convert claimed listings into paying members.** Move owners from free listing (listing only, no deals) → Growth ($39.99/mo) to post deals and reach the digest → Plus ($99.99/mo) for unlimited + featured with digest ad spots bundled in (à-la-carte those spots run Featured Deal $49/wk, Sponsor Spotlight $79/mo, Digest Banner $99/mo).

Simple way to remember it: **more neighbors reading, more businesses listed, more businesses paying.**

## 2. Who we're talking to

Two audiences. Same warm voice, different needs.

### a) Local shoppers in Lompoc & Vandenberg
- Live in ZIP 93436, 93437, or 93438.
- Want to save money, find something to do this weekend, and support their neighbors.
- Many speak Spanish at home — we engage in **both English and Spanish**.
- Busy. Scroll fast. Trust a familiar local name over a slick ad.

**What they care about:** real deals, honest reviews from neighbors, "what's new in town," family-friendly picks, and feeling proud of Lompoc.

### b) Small-business owners
- Restaurants, shops, salons, auto, wineries, services.
- Busy and **not very tech-savvy** — they run the register, cut the hair, fix the car.
- Skeptical of marketing that costs a lot and does little.

**What they care about:** getting found by the whole valley, filling slow days, simple and fair pricing, and not wasting time. Show them results, not dashboards.

## 3. Channels

Practical channels for a small local brand. For each: what it's for, and how to use it.

### Instagram + Facebook
- **For:** everyday visibility, deals, community pride, driving digest signups.
- **How:** Post 4–5x/week. Reels and photos of real local places beat graphics. Always caption in a way a neighbor would talk. Put the signup link in bio and pinned. Cross-post the same content to both, but write Facebook a touch longer.

### Local Facebook groups ("Lompoc community" pages)
- **For:** reaching neighbors who don't follow us yet.
- **How:** Be a helpful member first, not a billboard. Share a genuinely useful local guide or a great deal, tag the business, and let people ask. Answer every comment. Never spam the same link across groups in one day.

### Free weekly email digest
- **For:** our owned audience — the heart of everything. This is what businesses pay to be in.
- **How:** Send the same day and time every week (see cadence below). Lead with the best deals, add one Business Spotlight, one Local Guide link. Keep it skimmable. Every issue has a clear "Subscribe / forward to a neighbor" line and, for owners, a "List your business free" line. Digest also carries paid ad spots — keep them clearly local and tasteful.

### In-store flyers + QR codes
- **For:** turning foot traffic into subscribers, and showing owners we drive real people.
- **How:** Give member businesses a small counter flyer/window cling with a QR to their listing and to the digest signup. "Scan to see this week's deals." Bilingual card. It's free marketing for them and signups for us.

### Vandenberg SFB community boards
- **For:** reaching the base community — a big, loyal slice of the valley.
- **How:** Post on approved base/family community boards and spouse groups where allowed. Lead with family activities, dining deals, and services. Respect base posting rules; keep it clearly community-minded.

### Partnerships (Chamber, events, markets)
- **For:** trust and reach we can't buy.
- **How:** Join the Lompoc Valley Chamber of Commerce. Show up at the farmers market and local events with a table, flyers, and QR codes. Co-brand where it makes sense. A Chamber intro to a business owner converts far better than a cold message.

### Word-of-mouth / referrals
- **For:** the cheapest, strongest growth in a small town.
- **How:** Ask happy subscribers to forward the digest. Ask member businesses to refer a neighbor business. Small incentives (see Community tactics). In Lompoc, a recommendation from a friend beats any ad.

## 4. Content pillars & ideas

Four content pillars, tied to our four messaging pillars. Rotate them so the feed feels varied but on-brand.

### Pillar 1 — Community first
*Messaging tie: Community first.*
- **Community Shoutout:** "Neighbor of the week" — someone who volunteers, coaches, or keeps Lompoc great.
- Repost a local's photo of a Lompoc sunset or the flower fields with credit.
- Highlight a nonprofit fundraiser or blood drive happening this week.
- "Tag a neighbor who'd love this deal" prompts.

### Pillar 2 — Local & trusted
*Messaging tie: Local & trusted.*
- **Business Spotlight:** a short profile of one member business — how long they've been in town, what they're known for.
- **Behind-the-scenes / owner story:** "Meet the family behind [shop]." A 30-second Reel of the owner talking works great.
- "Family-owned in Lompoc since ___" posts.
- Before/after from a local salon, auto shop, or detailer.

### Pillar 3 — Real results (get found & sell deals)
*Messaging tie: Real results.*
- **Deal of the Week:** the single best deal, front and center. Bright, clear, one call to action.
- Roundup: "5 deals under $10 in Lompoc this week."
- **Local Guides:** "Best tacos in Lompoc," "Where to take the kids this weekend on Vandenberg," "Date night in Lompoc under $40," "Coffee stops before work."
- "New deal just posted" quick alerts.

### Pillar 4 — Simple & fair pricing (business-recruiting)
*Messaging tie: Simple & fair pricing.*
- "List your business free. Post deals from $39.99/mo." plain-and-clear graphic.
- Testimonial from an owner: "I filled my slow Tuesday."
- "How it works in 3 steps" carousel for owners.
- Myth-buster: "You don't need to be techy to post a deal. We'll help."

## 5. Weekly rhythm

### Sample 7-day cadence

| Day | Channel | Post type |
|---|---|---|
| **Monday** | IG + FB | Deal of the Week (Pillar 3) |
| **Tuesday** | IG + FB + a local FB group | Local Guide (e.g., best tacos) |
| **Wednesday** | IG + FB | Business Spotlight / owner story (Pillar 2) |
| **Thursday** | **Weekly email digest send** + IG/FB "digest is out" teaser | Digest + deals roundup |
| **Friday** | IG + FB | Weekend deals roundup / family activities |
| **Saturday** | IG Stories / FB | Community Shoutout + market/event coverage |
| **Sunday** | IG + FB (lighter) | Local pride / reshare a neighbor's photo |

Post at least one bilingual item (English + short Spanish) every week — usually the Deal of the Week or a Local Guide.

- Sun: Local pride reshare

**Week 2 — Family & Vandenberg focus**

- Thu: Digest send + Vandenberg community board post
- Fri: Weekend family activities roundup
- Sat: Community Shoutout (a coach, a nonprofit)
- Sun: Local pride reshare

**Week 3 — Beauty, auto & services focus**
- Mon: Deal of the Week (salon or auto)
- Tue: Guide — "Get ready for summer: local salons, detailers, more"
- Wed: Behind-the-scenes owner story (before/after)
- Thu: Digest send + business-recruiting note in issue

**Week 4 — Wineries, shops & shop-local focus**

Then the cycle repeats, refreshed with new businesses and deals.

## 7. Converting businesses

The funnel. Move owners one step at a time. Never oversell — show the next small win.

```
Claim free listing → Post first deal (Growth $39.99) → Reach the digest → Upgrade (Plus $99.99) → Top placement + bundled ad spots
```

### Step 1 — Claim your free listing
- **Ask:** "Get listed free. Takes 5 minutes. We'll help."
- **Nudge:** Show them their category is filling up — "12 Lompoc restaurants are already listed. Claim yours."
- We can pre-build a basic listing and invite the owner to claim it, so the first step feels done.

### Step 2 — Post your first deal (Growth, $39.99/mo)
- **Ask:** "You're listed — now post a deal and start showing up in front of shoppers."
- **Nudge:** "Free listings get found. Deals get sold. Your first deal reaches our weekly digest for $39.99/mo — cancel anytime."
- Remove the tech fear: offer to set up the first deal with them by phone or in person.

### Step 3 — Reach the digest / see results
- The Growth plan already puts them in the weekly digest. Reinforce the win: "Your Tuesday deal went out to the whole valley this morning."
- Share a simple result: views, clicks, or "your deal was one of this week's most-clicked."

### Step 4 — Upgrade to Plus ($99.99/mo)
- **Ask:** "Post as many deals as you want, get featured placement, and have your digest ad spots bundled in."
- **Nudge:** "You've posted deals all month and hit your limit. Plus is unlimited + featured, with digest ad spots bundled in (about $128/mo of value), for $99.99/mo — it pays for itself."

### Step 5 — Sponsor a featured spot (digest ads)
- **Featured Deal** — $49/wk: top of the digest for a week.
- **Sponsor Spotlight** — $79/mo: a dedicated spotlight block.
- **Digest Banner** — $99/mo: banner across the digest.
- **Ask:** "Want the whole valley to see you first? Sponsor next week's digest."
- Note: Plus members get a Featured Deal rotation + Sponsor Spotlight bundled in — so this step is mainly for Growth members or anyone adding a one-off Digest Banner.

### Outreach message snippets

## 8. Community tactics

Community is the brand. These build trust that ads can't.

- **Join the Chamber of Commerce.** Attend mixers. A Chamber intro converts owners far better than a cold call. Offer members a friendly onboarding.
- **Show up in person.** Table at the farmers market and local events with flyers and QR codes. Let people meet a face behind the brand. Sign up subscribers on the spot.
- **Spotlight nonprofits.** Feature a local nonprofit or fundraiser each month for free. Good for the town, good for us. It proves "community first."
- **Vandenberg community.** Support base family events and spouse-run businesses. This community is loyal and refers hard.
- **Referral incentives between businesses.** A member who refers another business that joins gets a free week of Featured Deal placement, or a month of Growth credit. Neighbors bringing neighbors.
- **Subscriber referrals.** "Forward this digest to a neighbor" every week. Occasional small perk — a shoutout, or entry into a local-business gift-card drawing.
- **Co-market with members.** Reshare their posts, tag them, celebrate their milestones ("[Business] just turned 10!"). When they win, we tag along.

Keep it strictly Lompoc + Vandenberg. Our whole promise is "all of Lompoc, in one place" — we don't wander outside the valley.

## 9. Metrics to watch

A short, honest list. Check weekly.

- **Digest subscribers** — total and net new each week. Our core audience number.
- **Digest open rate & click rate** — is the content landing? Watch which sections get clicks.
- **Listings claimed** — how many businesses are on the directory (free + paid).
- **Deals posted** — total live deals and new deals this week. Signals paid activity.
- **Paid conversions** — free → Growth, Growth → Plus, and ad-spot sales. This is revenue.
- **Top deals** — most-viewed and most-clicked deals each week. Use these as social proof for owners and as our best social content.
- **Social growth & engagement** — follower growth and saves/shares on IG + FB (a lighter signal, but tracks reach).

Rule of thumb: if subscribers are growing and deals posted is growing, the business is healthy. If one stalls, focus the next month there.
