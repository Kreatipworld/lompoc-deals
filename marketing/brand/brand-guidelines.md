# Lompoc Deals — Brand Guidelines
*Owner: CMO / Brand & Creative*
*Created: 2026-04-08 | Version: 1.0*

---

## Brand Purpose

**Why we exist:** To help Lompoc residents find and support local businesses — and give local businesses a free, bilingual way to reach their neighbors.

**One-sentence brand story:** Lompoc Deals connects neighbors with the best local deals, in their language, from businesses they already know.

---

## Brand Voice

### Personality
- **Local first** — We're from here. We know Lompoc.
- **Bilingual by default** — English and Spanish, always. Not as an afterthought.
- **Warm, not corporate** — Talk like a neighbor, not a brand.
- **Confident but humble** — We're building something real. No hype.
- **Direct** — "Save on your haircut this week" beats "Experience unparalleled value in the beauty vertical."

### Tone by Channel

| Channel | Tone |
|---------|------|
| Social (Instagram, TikTok, Facebook) | Casual, friendly, visual-first. Short sentences. Emojis OK. |
| Nextdoor | Neighborly, trust-building. No promotional language. |
| Email | Warm and personal. Subject lines: curiosity or value, never clickbait. |
| Merchant sales | Professional but not stiff. Lead with value, not features. |
| Press | Confident, data-led. "Local businesses" angle, not "startup" angle. |
| SEO pages | Clear, helpful, keyword-aware. Never write for robots. |

### Words We Use
- "local," "neighbors," "Lompoc," "your community"
- "free," "no credit card," "no commission"
- "claim," "browse," "support local"
- "bilingual," "en tu idioma / in your language"
- "deal," "special," "offer," "discount"

### Words We Avoid
- "leverage," "synergy," "ecosystem," "platform" (use "site" or "app" instead)
- "disrupt," "revolutionize"
- "users" (use "neighbors," "customers," "residents")
- Overly salesy: "AMAZING DEAL!!!", "LIMITED TIME OFFER!!!"
- Phrasing that implies Lompoc is small or limited — we celebrate it

## Visual Identity

### Colors

| Name | Hex | Use |
|------|-----|-----|
| **Lompoc Blue** | `#1D4ED8` | Primary CTAs, links, headers |
| **Warm Amber** | `#F59E0B` | Deal highlights, badges, accents |
| **Fresh White** | `#FFFFFF` | Backgrounds, card surfaces |
| **Soft Gray** | `#F3F4F6` | Secondary backgrounds, dividers |
| **Dark Charcoal** | `#111827` | Body text, headings |
| **Success Green** | `#10B981` | "Claimed," "Active," success states |
| **Alert Red** | `#EF4444` | Expiring soon, error states |

**Color rules:**
- Never use more than 3 colors in a single design piece
- Amber is for deals and highlights — use sparingly
- Blue is the primary brand color — use on key actions and headers

### Typography

**Headings:** Inter Bold or Semibold  
**Body:** Inter Regular  
**Accent / deal titles:** Inter Medium  

*Why Inter:* It's the font already in the product (Tailwind/shadcn default). Brand consistency = same font everywhere.

### Logo Usage

- Full logo: "Lompoc Deals" wordmark + icon (use on headers, splash screens)
- Icon only: "LD" monogram (use on social profile photos, favicons)
- Minimum size: 24px height for icon, 80px width for wordmark
- Clear space: 16px padding on all sides
- Do not stretch, recolor, or add drop shadows to the logo

### Imagery Style

- **Photos:** Real Lompoc businesses, real food, real people — not stock photos if avoidable
- **Illustrations:** Flat, simple, using brand colors — not 3D or hyper-realistic
- **Deal cards:** Always show real deal data — never placeholder text in mockups for public use
- **Video:** Natural light, handheld is fine, authentic > polished for social

## Bilingual Standards

Every consumer-facing piece of content must be available in both English and Spanish.

**Translation rules:**
- Use natural, conversational Spanish — not formal or machine-translated
- Match the tone (casual on social, warm on email)
- When in doubt: translate the feeling, not the words
- For marketing copy: EN version first, then ES — always check both make sense independently
- Avoid literal translations of idioms — find the Spanish equivalent

**Spanish voice guidance:**
- Use "tú" (informal) for consumer content
- Use "usted" (formal) for merchant sales outreach
- Lompoc-specific: lean toward Mexican Spanish pronunciation and phrasing (the dominant regional dialect)

## Messaging Framework

### For Consumers

**Headline options:**
- "Lompoc deals, in your language."
- "Your neighborhood. Your deals."
- "Save on the places you love in Lompoc."
- "Ofertas locales. Siempre gratis."

**Value proposition:**
1. Free to use — no credit card
2. Local businesses only — not national chains
3. Bilingual — English and Spanish
4. Easy to claim — just show your phone

### For Merchants

**Headline options:**
- "Reach Lompoc customers who are already looking for deals."
- "Free for local businesses. No commissions."
- "Your deal, in front of your neighbors."
- "Lompoc residents want to support you — make it easy."

**Value proposition:**
1. Free to list
2. No commission on claimed deals
3. Dashboard shows views + claims (proves ROI)
4. Bilingual listing — reach Spanish-speaking customers
5. Pro tier unlocks premium placement + advanced analytics

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do
- ✅ Lead every piece of content with the benefit, not the feature
- ✅ Use real deal examples from real Lompoc businesses in copy
- ✅ Tag and credit merchants in social posts
- ✅ Celebrate local milestones ("50 Lompoc businesses now on Lompoc Deals!")
- ✅ Post in both languages every time
- ✅ Keep URLs consistent: always lompoc-deals.vercel.app (until custom domain)

### Don't
- ❌ Promise features the CTO team hasn't shipped
- ❌ Use stock photos of generic people or food
- ❌ Post marketing copy that hasn't been reviewed for bilingual consistency
- ❌ Make claims we can't measure ("the best deals in town" without data)
- ❌ Use the brand colors or logo in ways that look unprofessional
- ❌ Commit to ad spend > $500 without CEO approval

## Brand Checklist (before publishing anything)

- [ ] Is it bilingual (EN + ES)?
- [ ] Does it use the correct brand colors and font?
- [ ] Is the URL correct (lompoc-deals.vercel.app)?
- [ ] Does it lead with benefit, not feature?
- [ ] Does it represent a feature that is actually live?
- [ ] Would a Lompoc neighbor find this useful or trust it?
- [ ] Has it been reviewed by CMO or Brand & Creative?
